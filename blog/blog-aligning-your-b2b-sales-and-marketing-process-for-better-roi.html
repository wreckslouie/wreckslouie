Aligning your B2B sales & marketing process for better ROI

The invisible line holding together the sales and marketing teams have always existed but it has become more obvious in recent years. To effectively grow your business and maximize your results, both the sales and marketing teams must align themselves.

“Highly aligned organisations achieve 38% lead to sales conversion rates. Misaligned organisations fail to follow up on 50% of marketing leads. And 79% never turn into sales.” - Blend (B2B Marketing Agency)

What exactly do you need to do to align these two teams? In today's B2B context, it means both the sales and marketing teams must be ready and able to respond to a buyer's action or behavior at any given time.

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

In order to align both teams together, you will need the correct processes, technology and people working together, in order to achieve the common goals. The first aspect of this alignment is to make sure that all processes are in place and the systems are being correctly used. An audit is imperative in learning if your marketing automation systems and Customer Relationship Management (CRM) fully functioning and not hindering the sales and marketing process.

This leads to the second point of making sure that all technology that is being employed is working together to generate the right data for lead generation. Taking time to settle the technological aspect might seem as a waste of time, but failing to do so will have a long-term impact on your business model.

People

Finally, the people. Without the people, all the processes and technology in the world will mean nothing if they are not on the same page. Both the sales and marketing teams need to be on the same page. Not only do they need to speak the same lingo, but a continuous communication flow is a must.

Marketing lingo or jargons might sometimes be confusing and that's why it's essential that both teams are on the same wave length. Constant two-way communication is also important, because either teams needs to be in constant know of where along the buying process is the client. Knowing this, they can tailor a more precise message to convert these leads.

Lastly, it's essential to have a detailed standard operating procedure (SOP). This will allow for successful lead generation and lead conversion to be replicated and copied, time after time. Keeping the details of the success will also allow for future benchmarking, ROI results and setting future goals.
