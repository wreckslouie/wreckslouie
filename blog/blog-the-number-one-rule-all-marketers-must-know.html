The number one rule all marketers must know

Rupen David Mannar

All marketers will understand the struggle. Coming up with creative strategies that would set your campaign apart from the competition, achieving your marketing team's ROIs all while keeping in mind the sales team's goals.

The problem we all face is that we sometimes tend to over think things. We want to be on the cutting edge of technology, we try to move traditional marketing practices for the digital platform and attempt to reach our target audience in a world of never-ending distractions.

According to Geoffrey James, a contributing editor at Inc.com, he faced this very struggle when he first started out in the marketing industry. He goes on to share that a wise man gave him these words of wisdom and it has changed the way he looks at marketing.

“Marketing is doing everything that makes buying easier.”

Such a simple phrase. Something that one might utter, without actually understanding the full weight of what they have just said. Essentially, what this phrase means is that, we marketers need to make the buying decision easier.

Now, the hidden secret that you might have just missed is that this means absolutely hogwash if you don't know who you are trying to reach. It's always about the customer. You need to help the potential buyer make his decision to purchase your product easier. So how do you do that?

Yes, everything you've learned in school about marketing and all you've picked up while on the job is important but it will mean nothing if it's not tailored to meet the customer's needs. If it doesn't ally their fears or reassure them, you're not making the buying process any easier.

Content marketing. We've all heard the term. But why is content marketing so important? Doesn't every campaign, advertisement and promotional banner already have “content”? Remember, just because you have words on a poster or on your ad, it doesn't mean that it's content.

The most important lesson to take away, when it comes to content marketing, is that it provides value. Carefully curated content can answer questions that buyers might have, provide important information that can reassure buyers that they are making the right decision and keep them satisfied with updates and tips even after their purchase.

So, if there is one thing we learn today is that the number one rule of marketing is that our job is to make the buying process easier. We need to offer direct answers that will persuade buyers to make that decision to purchase that product or service.
